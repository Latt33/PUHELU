## 1. Pharma-Funded COPD/Respiratory Screening Programs

### AstraZeneca: ACT on COPD
- **Structure:** Non-promotional, unbranded disease education and healthcare transformation program. Funded entirely by AstraZeneca.
- **Focus:** Proactive early identification of COPD patients, enabling timely initiation of evidence-based management. Provides HCP-facing educational resources, flare-up checklists based on GOLD criteria, and peer-led content.
- **Reach:** Operational globally with localized websites (US, Egypt, others).
- **Source:** [actoncopd.com](https://www.actoncopd.com/hcp.html)

### AstraZeneca: LungXperience Mobile Screening Tour (2024-2025)
- **Structure:** National mobile health initiative, partnership with Kroger Health and the American Lung Association. Features a 20x20-foot inflatable lung exhibit at NASCAR races, NHRA events, and state fairs.
- **Reach:** 75+ community events, projected audience of 1M+ people across the US.
- **Patient pathway:** Screening at events, opt-in to connect with American Lung Association Lung Health Navigators (trained respiratory nurses) who schedule appointments, arrange transportation, connect to tobacco cessation, and cover screening costs for uninsured/underinsured.
- **Key insight:** Turns awareness into measurable action through screening, referrals, and ongoing education.
- **Source:** [AstraZeneca US blog](https://www.astrazeneca-us.com/content/az-us/media/astrazeneca-us-blog/2025/building-pathways-to-better-lung-health-partnering-with-american-lung-association-to-drive-early-detection-and-timely-diagnosis-for-lung-cancer-and-copd.html), [PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/lungevity-foundation-joins-2025-lungxperience-tour-to-promote-lung-health-and-early-detection-across-the-us-302512393.html)

### AstraZeneca: RethinkCOPD
- **Structure:** Unbranded consumer-facing education campaign encouraging COPD patients to reconsider their treatment options (diet, exercise, medication).
- **Source:** [MM+M](https://www.mmm-online.com/home/channel/astrazeneca-unbranded-effort-urges-sufferers-to-rethink-copd/)

### AstraZeneca + Pangaea Data: AI-Powered EHR Screening
- **Structure:** Strategic collaboration using Pangaea's Pallux AI platform to scan electronic health records and identify untreated, undertreated, or misdiagnosed patients. Configured for 42+ conditions including COPD. Built on Microsoft Azure AI and Microsoft Fabric for secure data sharing.
- **Performance:** ~90% accuracy in finding at-risk COPD and CKD patients.
- **Relevance to DASH:** This is the closest precedent to the PUHELU model -- AI screening integrated into EHR workflows to flag undiagnosed COPD.
- **Source:** [MobiHealthNews](https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/exclusive-pangaea-data-astrazeneca-leverage-ai-detect-rare-and-hard-diagnose-diseases), [Pangaea Data](https://www.pangaeadata.ai/2025/12/02/astrazeneca-pangaea-partner-to-advance-ai-powered-precision-healthcare/)

### Boehringer Ingelheim: DRIVE4COPD (2010-)
- **Structure:** Multi-year public health initiative. Coalition of Boehringer Ingelheim, American Lung Association, COPD Foundation, and NASCAR.
- **Screening tool:** 5-question risk screener.
- **Reach:** Official Health Initiative of NASCAR. Celebrity ambassadors (Danica Patrick, Jim Belushi, Michael Strahan). 100,000+ screened in first 4 months; goal of 1M in first year.
- **Outcomes:** Generated mass awareness through sports events, cross-country tours across 14 cities.
- **Source:** [COPD Foundation](https://www.copdfoundation.org/About-Us/Past-Programs/DRIVE4COPD.aspx), [PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/drive4copd-sheds-light-on-the-nations-4th-leading-killer-through-unprecedented-public-health-initiative-83430182.html)

### CAPTURE Tool (Multi-Pharma Industry Advisory Committee)
- **Structure:** COPD Assessment in Primary Care To Identify Undiagnosed Respiratory Disease and Exacerbation Risk. 5-question screener + peak expiratory flow.
- **Funding:** Industry Advisory Committee includes Boehringer Ingelheim, AstraZeneca, Teva, Sunovion, GSK, and Viatris.
- **Scale:** 4,679 patients across 7 US primary care research networks.
- **Outcome:** Published in JAMA (2023). In pre-pandemic period, CAPTURE+ patients were more likely to complete spirometry referral; CAPTURE- patients saw reduced unnecessary COPD assessment. Net effect: more appropriate resource use.
- **Source:** [JAMA](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2801317), [COPD Foundation](https://www.copdfoundation.org/Research/Completed-Studies-Results-and-Updates/CAPTURE.aspx)

### GSK: Joint Working Projects (UK NHS)
- **Greater Manchester:** Partnership with Health Innovation Manchester. LungHealth software provides algorithm-guided COPD consultation in line with NICE guidelines.
  - Results: 212 new COPD diagnoses across 48 GP practices. CAT score recording increased from 7.2% to 69.7%. 67% of existing patients underwent clinical review.
  - **Source:** [Health Innovation Manchester](https://healthinnovationmanchester.com/our-work/gsk-health-innovation-manchester-copd/)
- **Berkshire West:** 23 practices. GOLD risk stratification + structured education + LungHealth software.
  - **Source:** [GSK outcomes report](https://www.gsk.com/media/bpefu5pn/berkshire-west-summary-of-outcomes.pdf)

### GSK: Salford Lung Study
- **Structure:** World's first large-scale randomized controlled trial conducted entirely in real-world primary care, published in NEJM. 2,802 COPD patients across 80 GP practices, using EHR data for monitoring.
- **Results:** 8.4% reduction in moderate/severe exacerbation rate (p=0.025).
- **Significance:** Proved that pharma-funded RWE generated through primary care EHR integration can produce NEJM-publishable evidence.
- **Source:** [NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1608033), [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5614786/)

### UNANIME Pilot Study (France)
- **Structure:** Multi-provider screening involving dentists, pharmacists, physiotherapists, nurses, and GPs.
- **Results:** 22% of 157 high-risk participants had FEV1/FEV6 abnormalities. 43% engaged in smoking cessation at 2-month follow-up. 14% quit smoking.
- **Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2590041221000428)

## 2. Pharma-Funded Diagnostic Programs in Other Disease Areas

### Rare Disease: Sponsored Genetic Testing Programs
Multiple pharma companies fund free genetic testing for patients meeting clinical criteria:
- **X4 Pharmaceuticals + Invitae:** Congenital neutropenia diagnosis
- **PTC Therapeutics + Invitae:** Cerebral palsy diagnosis
- **GeneDx + Krystal Biotech:** Rare skin disease diagnosis
- **Takeda (Baxalata) + PerkinElmer:** Lysosomal storage disorder diagnosis in India
- **Structure:** Pharma covers testing cost; diagnostic company provides service; patient advocacy groups promote awareness.
- **Source:** [3billion.io](https://3billion.io/blog/pharma-rare-disease-diagnostic-companies-collaborating)

### Quest Diagnostics: Commercial Sponsored Testing
- **Structure:** Day-one lab testing readiness, education, awareness, and support programs so patients can receive therapy without delay.
- **Source:** [Quest Diagnostics](https://www.questdiagnostics.com/business-solutions/life-sciences/pharma-solutions/commercialization-and-sponsored-testing)

### Pfizer: PHQ-9 and GAD-7 Development
- **Structure:** Pfizer funded development and validation of the PHQ-9 (depression screener) and GAD-7 (anxiety screener), which became global standard screening tools.
- **Impact:** PHQ-9 validation article cited 3,500+ times. Most-cited article ever in Journal of General Internal Medicine.
- **Significance:** Shows how pharma-funded screening tool development can become embedded in standard clinical practice worldwide.

### OIG Advisory Opinions (US Legal Precedent)
- **AO 25-07 (2025):** OIG approved pharma manufacturer offering free companion lab test to eligible patients where the test is a medically necessary prerequisite for safe prescribing. Key safeguard: manufacturer's field personnel do not discuss the drug when talking about the testing program.
- **AO 24-12:** Approved free genetic testing + counseling for ultra-rare genetic condition. Requires HCP attestation that patient meets clinical/family history criteria.
- **Enforcement case:** Ultragenyx paid $6M to settle allegations of illegal remuneration through free genetic testing (2023) -- demonstrating the line between compliant and non-compliant programs.
- **Source:** [National Law Review](https://natlawreview.com/article/oig-advisory-opinion-25-07-offers-favorable-path-patient-access-through-sponsored), [Faegre Drinker](https://www.faegredrinker.com/en/insights/publications/2025/3/the-latest-on-pharmaceutical-company-sponsored-diagnostic-testing-programs)

## 3. Legal/Ethical Compliance Frameworks

### How Programs Stay Within Ethical and Legal Limits

**Structural safeguards across compliant programs:**
1. **Unbranded / non-promotional:** Disease education is separated from product promotion. No brand names in screening materials.
2. **Independent medical education (IME):** Content, faculty, and agenda are under sole control of the accredited education provider, not the funder.
3. **Firewall between field force and screening:** Sales reps do not participate in or discuss screening programs. Medical affairs runs them.
4. **HCP-initiated enrollment:** Patients are enrolled through their healthcare provider, not through direct pharma outreach.
5. **Fair market value:** Any compensation to HCPs for services is documented, at FMV, and not tied to referrals.
6. **Transparency and disclosure:** All funding sources disclosed. Transfers of value reported per EFPIA/national codes.
7. **No promotional messaging:** Programs cannot promote prescription, supply, or consumption of specific medicines.
8. **Data minimization:** GDPR-compliant data handling with explicit consent and opt-out mechanisms.

### EFPIA Code (Europe-wide)
- Self-regulatory framework for pharmaceutical promotion and HCP/HCO/patient organization interactions.
- **EFPIA Patient Solutions Guidelines (December 2024):** New annex establishing rules for any pharma-funded service, program, or solution benefiting individual patients. Key rules:
  - Must demonstrate documented medical need
  - Cannot include inducements for HCPs to prescribe specific products
  - Must not interfere with physician-patient relationships
  - Patient enrollment through HCPs, not direct outreach
  - No remuneration or personal benefits for patients
- **EFPIA Disclosure Code:** Requires transparent reporting of all transfers of value to HCPs and HCOs.
- **Source:** [EFPIA Code](https://www.efpia.eu/relationships-code/the-efpia-code/), [EFPIA Patient Solutions Guidelines](https://www.ipha.ie/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/EFPIA-Patient-Solutions-Guidelines-Final-20241219.pdf)

### Finnish Pharma Industry Code (Lääketeollisuus ry / PIF)
- PIF Code of Ethics is binding for all member companies in Finland.
- **Based on:** Finnish medicine/competition/consumer legislation + EFPIA guidelines + IFPMA code.
- **Disease awareness rules:** Health awareness information must focus on the disease itself and diagnostics, NOT therapy options. Must be matter-of-fact, reliable, high-quality, and in good taste.
- **Consumer information:** Objective is to help consumers recognize diseases, symptoms, and risk factors. Premise must be the disease, not treatment promotion.
- **Enforcement:** Supervisory Commission for the Marketing of Medicinal Products monitors compliance. Breaches can lead to considerable sanction payments.
- **Source:** [PIF Code of Ethics 2023](https://www.alzheimer-europe.org/sites/default/files/2023-03/pharma_industry_finland_code_of_ethics_2023.pdf), [PIF Marketing](https://www.pif.fi/responsibility/marketing), [PIF Supervision](https://www.pif.fi/responsibility/marketing/supervision-of-pharmaceutical-marketing)

### European Transparency Requirements
- Reporting obligations for transfers of value from pharma to HCPs/HCOs are based on both laws and self-regulatory industry codes.
- EU Clinical Trial Regulation (applicable since January 2022) mandates transparency of clinical trial data.
- **Source:** [MediSpend](https://www.medispend.com/europe-transparency-reporting/)

## 4. Benefits to Pharma Beyond Direct Medication Revenue

### 4a. Real-World Evidence (RWE) Generation
- Screening programs generate patient-level data on disease prevalence, diagnostic pathways, and treatment patterns.
- RWE is now used in ~50% of FDA drug approvals (2020 data).
- Professional organizations sell RWD to pharma R&D and marketing departments, reducing clinical trial costs and improving strategic planning.
- **Salford Lung Study** (GSK) demonstrated that EHR-integrated primary care data can produce NEJM-publishable RWE.
- **Source:** [FDA RWE](https://www.fda.gov/science-research/science-and-research-special-topics/real-world-evidence), [Deloitte](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/health-care/real-world-evidence-study.html)

### 4b. Health Economics and Outcomes Research (HEOR) Data
- HEOR data demonstrates economic value of treatments to payers and health systems.
- Screening programs provide unique data on: cost of underdiagnosis, disease burden in untreated populations, cost-effectiveness of early intervention, budget impact of improved screening.
- With tightening budgets, policymakers increasingly rely on HEOR to guide formulary and funding decisions.
- **Source:** [pharmaphorum](https://pharmaphorum.com/views-and-analysis/health-economics-and-outcomes-research-heor-whats-the-value), [Viseven](https://viseven.com/heor-pharma/)

### 4c. Market Shaping / Disease Awareness
- Unbranded disease state education (DSE) campaigns shape HCP and patient understanding of a condition before or alongside product launch.
- US disease awareness ad spending grew from $177M (1997) to $430M (2016); campaigns grew from 44 to 401.
- Creates favorable conditions for treatment adoption without direct product promotion.
- **Source:** [Fierce Pharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/marketing/did-you-know-dtc-disease-awareness-soars-u-s-spending-and-placement-up), [ZoomRx](https://blog.zoomrx.com/brand-builder/strategic-insights-for-unbranded-disease-education-campaigns/)

### 4d. Health System Partnerships / Key Account Management
- 42% year-over-year increase in health system leaders' desire to partner with manufacturers post-COVID.
- 90% of pharma leaders say KAM is a strategic priority.
- Screening programs position pharma as a health system partner, not just a vendor. Value propositions include: improved care pathways, reduced hospital admissions, EHR integration support.
- Shifts relationship from transactional (drug sales) to strategic (shared outcomes).
- **Source:** [Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/healthcare-life-sciences/life-sciences-software/pharma-key-account-management/), [PharmExec](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/why-pharma-must-invest-in-key-account-management-capabilities)

### 4e. Data for Regulatory Submissions
- RWD from screening registries supports: natural history documentation, unmet need evidence, disease burden quantification, safety/effectiveness in real-world populations.
- Disease-based multi-drug registries collect regulatory-grade longitudinal data.
- Screening data can be used in label extension applications and HTA submissions.
- **Source:** [PPD](https://www.ppd.com/our-solutions/clinical/real-world-evidence/observational-studies/registry-studies/)

### 4f. Publications and Academic Partnerships
- Pharma-funded screening tools (PHQ-9, GAD-7, CAPTURE) have become standard clinical instruments.
- Publication in high-impact journals (NEJM, JAMA, Lancet) generates credibility and citation impact.
- Academic partnerships provide independent validation and KOL engagement.
- Risk: industry-funded studies have OR 4.05 of favorable outcomes vs. independent studies -- requires strong independent academic governance.
- **Source:** [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4720652/), [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC156458/)

## 5. Nordic/Finnish Precedents

### FinnGen Project
- **Scale:** EUR 59M public-private partnership to genotype 500,000 Finnish biobank samples.
- **Public funding:** EUR 20M from Business Finland (formerly Tekes).
- **Pharma partners:** AstraZeneca, AbbVie, Biogen, Bristol-Myers Squibb, Genentech, GlaxoSmithKline, Janssen, Maze Therapeutics, MSD, Pfizer, Sanofi (11 companies).
- **Academic partner:** Institute for Molecular Medicine Finland (FIMM), University of Helsinki.
- **Outputs:** 700,000 genomic variants, 16M estimated genotypes, 1,800+ disease endpoints analyzed. Results published openly after 1-year embargo.
- **Governance model:** Academic independence maintained; pharma gets pre-publication data access; all results eventually public.
- **Relevance:** Proves Finnish regulatory/ethical environment supports large-scale pharma-public PPPs. Shows pharma willingness to invest tens of millions for population-level health data in Finland specifically.
- **Source:** [GenomeWeb](https://www.genomeweb.com/genetic-research/finnish-researchers-pharma-partners-genotype-500k-samples-part-new-59m-study), [FinnGen](https://www.finngen.fi/en/partners), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FinnGen)

### FINPROVE: Precision Cancer Medicine
- **Structure:** Public-private partnership across Finland's five university hospital cancer centers (FICANs).
- **Pharma partners:** Roche, Novartis, Bayer, Janssen, Eli Lilly.
- **Focus:** Molecular diagnostics infrastructure, data storage, reimbursement pathways, and a national precision medicine trial.
- **Source:** [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11332475/)

### Nordic Pharmacoepidemiological Network (NorPEN)
- Cross-Nordic network facilitating pharmaceutical epidemiology research using Nordic health registries.
- Nordic registries are recognized globally as gold-standard real-world data sources.
- **Source:** [EMA Catalogues](https://catalogues.ema.europa.eu/network/2221211)

### Finnish Healthcare Infrastructure Advantages
- Universal EHR coverage (Apotti/Epic in Helsinki, Lifecare in 14 regions, OMNI360 in 4, Esko in northern regions).
- All systems FHIR-capable.
- National personal identity number enables longitudinal data linkage.
- Biobank Act enables research use of health data with appropriate governance.
- **Relevance:** Finland is uniquely positioned for EHR-integrated screening pilots because of centralized digital infrastructure.

## 7. Compliance Framework Summary for DASH/PUHELU

Based on the precedents above, a compliant pharma-funded screening program in Finland should:

| Requirement | How DASH satisfies it |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Unbranded** | DASH is disease-focused (COPD screening), not product-focused |
| **Disease-centric premise** (PIF Code) | Screening focuses on COPD risk identification, not treatment options |
| **HCP-initiated** (EFPIA) | Doctor receives the flag and decides on action; patient does not receive a pharma message |
| **No promotional messaging** | CAT score and vocal biomarker are clinical tools, not brand communications |
| **Transparent scoring** (PIF Code: "matter-of-fact, reliable") | DASH uses transparent GOLD 2023 scoring; no black-box output |
| **Sensitivity > specificity** | False positives = doctor pays attention; false negatives = missed COPD -- aligns with screening ethics |
| **EHR integration** | Data stays in existing clinical workflow (Apotti); no separate pharma-owned system |
| **Data minimization** (GDPR) | Audio deleted immediately after processing; only clinical scores retained |
| **Medical need** (EFPIA Patient Solutions) | COPD 75-90% underdiagnosis rate is documented unmet medical need |
| **Independent academic governance** | University of Helsinki / Aalto partnership ensures independent validation |

### Key Codes to Reference
1. **EFPIA Code of Practice** + Patient Solutions Guidelines (Dec 2024)
2. **PIF Code of Ethics** (Lääketeollisuus ry) -- specifically sections on health awareness information and consumer disease information
3. **Finnish Medicines Agency (Fimea)** regulatory oversight
4. **GDPR** -- data minimization, explicit consent, right to erasure
5. **Finnish Biobank Act** -- if collecting research data
6. **EFPIA Disclosure Code** -- transparency of transfers of value

## Key Takeaways for PUHELU Business Case

1. **There is extensive precedent** for pharma funding COPD screening programs. AstraZeneca alone operates 8+ such programs globally.

2. **The closest direct precedent to PUHELU is AstraZeneca + Pangaea Data** -- AI-powered EHR screening for undiagnosed COPD with ~90% accuracy. PUHELU adds a novel vocal biomarker layer.

3. **Finland is already a pharma PPP-friendly environment.** FinnGen (EUR 59M, 11 pharma partners) and FINPROVE demonstrate that Finnish regulators, biobanks, and health systems support large-scale pharma partnerships.

4. **Screening programs generate multi-layered value for pharma:**
   - RWE for regulatory submissions and label extensions
   - HEOR data for payer negotiations
   - Market shaping through disease awareness
   - Health system relationship building (KAM)
   - Academic publications and KOL networks
   - Population-level epidemiological data

5. **Compliance is achievable** through unbranded design, HCP-initiated pathways, transparent scoring, academic independence, and adherence to EFPIA + PIF codes.

6. **The NEJM precedent matters.** GSK's Salford Lung Study proved that EHR-integrated primary care data from a pharma-funded program can produce top-tier publishable evidence. PUHELU could offer similar publication potential.
